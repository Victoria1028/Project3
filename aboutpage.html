Neville Brody was born on April 23, 1957 in Southgate, London

He started his early education at Michenden Grammar School, where he took a strong interest in art.

In 1975, Brody began his college career by attending the Hornsey College of Art for Fine Art foundational course.

In 1981, Brody officially became the Art Director for a magazine, "The Face".

In 1991, Brody and fellow designer Jon Wozencroft founded the FUSE project.

In 2011, Brody served as the Dean for the School of Communication at Londonâ€™s Royal College of Art. During his time, he remodeled and evolved the school's programs, as well as created "two new additions to the college's portfolio."

In 2018, he stepped down as the Dean to allow someone new to take his place in the school's next phase of development. He became a Professor of Visual Communication, where he remains to this day.

Today, Neville Brody is a world-renowned designer, typographer, and teacher. He is the judge for the Dezeen Awards and works closely with his company Brody Associates.
